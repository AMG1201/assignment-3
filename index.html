OPPORTUNITES


WHAT ARE THE OPPORTUNITIES FOR SOCIETY REGARDING SERVICE AUTOMATION?

The world is becoming more and more automated as the years go on. According to Forbes, a survey done by Deloitte, half of the people that were surveyed said that their organization is “deeply involved in automation projects, with 24% using AI and robotics to perform routine tasks” (Arka Dhar, 2019). This is where automation in service industries opens up a lot of opportunities. There are many opportunities that service automation opens up in travel, tourism and hospitality companies. In hotels employee jobs such as checking in to the hotel are taken over by service automation. Employee front desk jobs are taken over regarding check ins by mobile apps and other software which make it easier for customers staying at the hotel to have a contactless, fast check in (MGM Resorts International, 2017). Other services are now inclining more and more towards robotics. The hotel Henn-na, is the first fully robotically automated hotel which replaces front desk workers, their front desk is operated by 3 robots. Other services are also robotically automated such as robotic vacuum cleaners, and even a robotic arm that operates the luggage storage room (http://www.h-n-h.jp/en/) to achieve “ultimate efficiency”. Other industries are also moving towards automated service.

Many restaurants, especially sushi restaurants, are implementing conveyor belts to get their food to their customers who are sitting outside it (Ngai, Suk, & Lo, 2008). This makes it easier for workers to serve their customers without leaving their workstation, cutting down on time. This can also be a unique selling point (UPS) for a business where they can possibly sell their food for a slightly higher price using the conveyor belt as a UPS. In the flights and travel industry service automation has been significantly implemented to cut down the travel time in airports as well as cutting down costs of labor. For example in 2016 Auckland has implemented their first check in kiosk to prepare for the high volume of travelers during the summer holidays. Auckland airport has invested in 45 of these Kiosk Self check-in machines where customers are able to check in, print their ticket, get the tags for their bags and drop it off (Gez Johns, 2016). Furthermore the opportunities for service automation at airports continue to increase, we see this where the luggage of the passengers are taken directly to the airplane without a middleman to carry the luggage to the airplane or a vehicle to tug it to the airplane. There is then a worker in the belly of the plane sorting and ready to store it. A regular belt loader can be extended with the Rollertrack Conveyor. It reaches into the bulk hold of the aircraft, turns 90 degrees, and delivers bags to the ramp agent in the cargo hold promptly and reliably.

The Rollertrack Conveyor system allows airport ground handlers to load and unload large baggage and freight more quickly and effectively, while also minimizing physical strain on the employees (https://powerstow.com/rollertrack-conveyor/). There are other industries where the opportunity for service automation is very limited as physical machines are not yet implemented in industries like tourism and travel agencies. For example in the tourism industry there is a voiceover automation when visiting tourist sites rather than a worker being physically there. But this cannot always be automated by machines as some tourism areas require workers to be there. For example tourist sites which are natural and robotics and machinery cannot be accessed i.e Glow worm caves. Other industries such as travel agencies are automated using third party hosts such as the internet, google etc. There are no physical machines at the travel agencies tending to customers rather a website online. Although it is partially automated it is not fully automated and it does take some human resources to then complete the service. Automated services through AI can't always be implemented in every service and every stage of sales, eventually it does require human resources to fully implement those services. Advanced robotic technology paired with human resources and human interaction is the best option for automating services in the future.

RISKS


Automation can be very useful and helpful in today’s modern day society. It can help people accomplished everyday tasks at a faster rate and accomplish those tasks easier. However, despite how helpful Automation can be, it also has negative effects on people and also in our society.

NEGATIVE EFFECTS OF SERVICE AUTOMATION

Job Obsolescence and Unemployment
With the rise of globalization and advancements in technology, many companies have switched to automation to remain competitive. Millions of jobs have been lost to automation and machines are taking people’s job.

High Cost of Invest in Automation
An automated system may cost thousands to millions of dollars to outline, build, and install. Some automated tools necessitate more maintenance than traditional machinery. As a result, small manufacturing companies may be hesitant to invest in an automated solution when they already have machinery that works.

Limited Capacity of Machines
Though machinery is less prone to human error for some processes, there is a downside to using technology in place of a person. A person can think critically, respond more flexibly to situations, and use creativity to solve problems. A machine is limited by pre-programmed inputs and is less able to solve complex situations.

Security Concerns
In a data-driven world, businesses are harnessing valuable information to attract new customers, increase sales, and improve productivity. Managing thousands of paper documents, receipts, and transactions is just as susceptible to theft and fraud. Businesses are forced to rely on locks and security measures to protect valuable records and financial information’s.

POSITIVE EFFECTS OF SERVICE AUTOMATION

Reduction of Repetitive Tasks
The repetition of daily tasks is common when the work processes are manual. When the service automation system is implemented, the tasks are delegated to the machines, therefore the employees can focus on strategic activities for the business.

Reduce Human Errors
When people do mundane tasks over and over, errors are bound to happen but with service automation, you can easily integrate your existing processes and data into a secured dashboard.

Increase Operational Efficiency
Service automation streamlines processes, opening the door for time and cost savings in many ways by automating routine tasks and creating more efficient workflows, facilities managers can focus on those tasks that truly need attention, directly impacting the operational effectiveness of the team.

Improve Customer Satisfaction
Service automation allows easier communication between facilities staff which translates to happier customers. It ensures that work is done in a high-quality and timely manner and customers don’t experience any interruptions that would affect their in-store experience.

Automation can be quite a controversial topic; it contains both positives and negatives and there is no clear morally right point of view on the topic when arguing for and against it. An example of this is automation when it comes to monitoring employees' job performance. For businesses to reach their goals and be profitable, they need to ensure that their employees are on track with their work. While there is nothing wrong with keeping track of an employee's job performance, through the use of extensive observation with the use of automation it can have quite a negative effect. With the use of digital techniques, management can monitor all use of time including inefficient and time-wasting behavior such as social media. According to (Baecker, Computers and society: Modern perspectives 2019), a study showed that organizations that used computerized monitoring increased organization control, productivity, and assessment of employee performance. But it also increased stress levels and employees had a higher job dissatisfaction compared to organizations that did not use computerized monitoring. (Baecker, Computers and society: Modern perspectives 2019). “The more employers relied upon CPM data to judge job performance; the more dissatisfied employees were with evaluation. Employee stress increased with the degree to which job task monitoring results were reported to them” (Baecker, Computers and society: Modern perspectives 2019). So, while Automation allows businesses to monitor employees more which has shown its benefits to the business, it harms the employees more.

In more recent times, particularly in the service industry where delivery is involved.
